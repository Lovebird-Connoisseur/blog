It seems a bit late for a post new years resolution.

To be honest I've had my objectives written down for a couple of weeks prior, but my natural laziness, combined with exam period prevented me from organizing all of this in a clear manner.

I usually don't bother with things like these, but this last semester has gone surprisingly well for me, reminding me once again that, with enough willpower and work nothing's impossible.

So, without further ado, here are my goals for the year of 2025:

Finish the year with good grades

I never considered myself a particularly brilliant student. I didn't bother studying until I was in 10th grade (or sophomore, as it seems to be called in the US).

Even then I only studied enough to pass, school wasn't something I enjoyed and during that time I was going through a transitionary period in my life1, which didn't help my situation.

Things changed when I went to retake my math exam, a year after getting out of school, I went from knowing practically zero about math to dominating it2.

And then came university and… well, I wouldn't call my first nor my second year perfect, in fact I felt pretty lost during those first 2 years and even wondered if Computer Science was right for me.

This last semester felt different, for the first time I finally felt like I was starting to make sense of all of this.

Focusing on achieving good grades may not be the most efficient method to get the hang of a subject, but at least it ensures that I understand what has been taught.

Still, I'd be lying if I claimed to be satisfied with my current proportions. Particularly the size around my abdomen.

Do more leisure activities

Self explanatory, I wasn't the most organized last semester and my free time took a hit.

By leisure activities I mean things like: Gaming, watching movies, going for walks…

Go for a walk more often

There's nothing more relaxing than going for a walk. Especially when one lives so close to the sea and city park.

Alas, this was another thing I sacrificed last semester. I should arrange to go outside at least twice per week.

Learn how to ride a bike (and acquire one)

Okay, this one isn't really an objective per see, in fact I already know how to ride a bike!3

Although I think getting the hang of it to the point where I'm road safe proficient enough to ride alongside cars would be a good idea.

Besides, I believe purchasing a bike would a good choice, both in terms of personal freedom as well as giving me another excuse to go outside.

Be more informed

I can't help but feel like I've shut myself off the outside world these past few months, consequently, I find that I lack enough context to form my own opinions about current world affairs as well as national issues. Thus:

  • I'm more prone to misinformation and less likely to identify it.
  • I'm less informed and thus more likely to make "bad" choices during referendums and elections.

As a means to counter these issues, I've decided to:

  • Get a yearly subscription of a national newspaper.
  • Get a library card, giving me free access to a ton of magazines and newspaper through Press Reader.
  • Start following parliament and municipal council votes, parish council reunions and government issued laws.
  • Read more books, especially ones related to political science and history.

Work on my style

Pretty much all my clothes have been gifted to me over the years.

With the passage of time some of these items have worn/damaged, meanwhile others have become too loose or tight. Even ignoring this, most pieces of clothing don't match my style anymore, either due to age related reasons or simply because I find them plain ugly.

After spending quite a bit of time around online male fashion communities I believe I've gotten my style down, with the only remaining thing left to do being actually buying the clothes themselves.

Although this may prove to be a bit more challenging than initially anticipated, due to my interest in losing weight.

Reduce phone and social media usage

The smartphone was, by far, my biggest distraction this semester, frequently preventing me from focusing on classes and taking away my attention when going for a walk or waiting for something. 4

Instead of ditching the smartphone entirely 5, I've chosen to take a middle of the road solution, where I've hidden the browser (by far the biggest time sink) and use some extensions to limit time wasting sites (mainly social media).

As for why I want to reduce social media usage, I already went over that topic in my first article.
